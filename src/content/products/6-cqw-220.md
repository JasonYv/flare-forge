## CCS-Certified for Classed Vessels Worldwide

The AV-MAR-220 (Lingyun **6-CQW-220(1200)**) carries one of the hardest-to-obtain certifications in the battery industry: **CCS (China Classification Society) Type Approval**. This certification — held by very few Chinese battery makers — opens the door to commercial marine fitments on classed vessels: cargo ships, tankers, fishing fleets, and offshore service vessels.

### Why CCS Approval Matters

Commercial marine insurance and class society rules (CCS, ABS, DNV, Lloyd's, BV, ClassNK) require that critical onboard equipment — including starting and emergency batteries — carry type approval from a recognized class society. Without it:

- Insurance claims may be denied
- Vessels can fail port-state inspections
- Charterers may reject the vessel

CCS approval is mutually recognized by ABS, DNV, BV, and other IACS member societies for many applications, making this battery acceptable on classed vessels operating internationally.

### Marine Duty Cycle is Brutal

A marine battery faces challenges no automotive battery sees:

- **24/7 vibration** from main engine and hull pounding
- **Salt-laden humidity** that destroys terminals in months
- **Combined duty cycle** — cranking the main engine + powering nav lights, radio, fridge, autopilot for days
- **Charging from variable sources** — alternator, solar, shore power, genset

The 6-CQW platform is built specifically for this combination.
