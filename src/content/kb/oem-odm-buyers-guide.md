## OEM vs ODM vs Private Label — what's actually different

Industry uses these terms loosely. Here's how to read between the lines:

| Term | What you control | What the factory controls | Typical MOQ |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Private Label** | Branding only (label, box) | Design, specifications, formula | 1 × 40HQ container |
| **OEM** | Branding + spec adjustments | Core design, manufacturing | 5+ containers |
| **ODM** | Brand + functional requirements | Full design and engineering | 20+ containers |

Most "OEM" deals in lead-acid batteries are actually private label — you pick a stock product, put your brand on it, and ship. True OEM with custom specs starts at much higher volumes.

## What you can customize at private-label volumes

Even at the lowest MOQ tier (1 container), you can customize:

- **Battery label** — your brand, color scheme, model code, country of origin marking
- **Top cover plug colors** (if applicable)
- **Carton box** — your design, your colors, your barcoding
- **Pallet wrap and shipping marks** — your destination labeling
- **MSDS and certificates** — re-issued under your brand name (with manufacturer disclosed)

## What requires OEM-level commitment

For physical product changes, expect higher MOQs and tooling investment:

- **Custom case color** — typically 10+ containers per color (mold cleaning + setup time)
- **Custom case dimensions** — full mold development, $15,000–$80,000 tooling fee, 60–90 day lead time
- **Specific CCA / capacity tuning** — adjusting plate count or active material formula, requires testing
- **Custom terminal type** — limited to existing tooling, special types require investment
- **Embedded BMS / smart battery** — separate engineering project

## MOQ benchmarks

Volume requirements vary by product type:

- **Standard automotive (47–105Ah)** — 1 × 40HQ ≈ 504 pcs
- **Heavy-duty truck (135–200Ah)** — 1 × 40HQ ≈ 80–120 pcs
- **Solar VRLA (100–200Ah)** — 1 × 40HQ ≈ 64–100 pcs
- **Marine** — 1 × 40HQ ≈ 40–60 pcs (heavy units)

Mixed loads are possible at slightly higher unit price.

## Lead times you should plan for

| Stage | Typical Duration |
|---|---|
| Initial quotation | 1–2 days |
| Sample production (first deal) | 15–30 days |
| Sample shipping (air to importer) | 5–10 days |
| Importer testing & approval | 14–30 days |
| Production (after PO + LC opening) | 30–45 days |
| Sea freight (China → destination) | 25–55 days |
| **Total first-order pipeline** | 90–170 days |

For repeat orders, the pipeline shrinks to 60–90 days.

## Payment terms

Common patterns for OEM private-label deals:

- **30% T/T deposit + 70% T/T against B/L copy** (most common, lowest fee)
- **30% T/T + 70% L/C at sight** (second most common, distributor protection)
- **L/C 100% at sight** (rare, only for largest accounts)
- **D/P, D/A** (very rare, factory risk too high)

Open Account / Net 60 terms are unusual in lead-acid battery export — too much working capital risk for the factory.

## Pitfalls to avoid

### 1. Specifying without seeing the actual product

Always request samples before committing to an MOQ. Test them in your target application, in your target climate, for 30+ days minimum.

### 2. Underestimating the freight piece

Lead-acid batteries are heavy. A 40HQ container weighs out, not cubes out. Verify max gross weight per container limits at your destination port.

### 3. Skipping the third-party inspection

For first-time deals, hire a third-party inspector (SGS, Bureau Veritas, or local) to verify production before shipment. $400–$800 well spent vs. a $40,000 bad container.

### 4. Underestimating warranty claim handling

Set warranty terms upfront in writing — % defect rate covered, replacement vs credit, who pays return freight (typically destruction-in-place with photos rather than physical return).

### 5. Using wrong incoterms

FOB Shanghai/Ningbo/Qingdao is most common and gives the buyer maximum flexibility. CFR includes ocean freight (factory's negotiated rate vs your forwarder's). DDP requires the factory to handle import customs — typically not feasible cross-border.
