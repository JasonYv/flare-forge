## The regulatory framework

International lead-acid battery shipments are governed by:

- **IMDG Code** (International Maritime Dangerous Goods) — for ocean freight
- **IATA DGR** (Dangerous Goods Regulations) — for air freight
- **49 CFR** — US Department of Transportation rules
- **ADR** — European road transport
- **UN Recommendations** — global model regulations

For lead-acid batteries the relevant UN numbers are:

- **UN2794** — Wet, filled with acid, electric storage batteries (most automotive batteries)
- **UN2795** — Wet, filled with alkali, electric storage batteries
- **UN2800** — Wet, non-spillable, electric storage batteries (sealed AGM/VRLA)
- **UN3028** — Dry, with potassium hydroxide solid, electric storage batteries

## What you should receive with every shipment

A compliant lead-acid battery shipment from China should include:

### 1. Material Safety Data Sheet (MSDS / SDS)

Latest version (16-section GHS format), in English plus the destination country language if required. The MSDS must show:
- Product identification
- Hazard classification (Class 8 corrosive)
- First aid measures
- Handling and storage
- Transport information (UN2794 / UN2800)

### 2. UN packaging certification

Lead-acid batteries are typically shipped under **special provision 238**, which exempts properly packaged spillable batteries from full Class 8 packaging requirements *if* they:

- Are stowed and packed to prevent short circuits
- Have terminal protection
- Are protected against falling and damage
- Are loaded in a manner that prevents leakage

Sealed VRLA batteries (UN2800) are non-spillable and have a simpler shipping classification.

### 3. Container loading certificate

For ocean freight, FCL container shipments need:
- **Container packing certificate** signed by the shipper
- **Dangerous goods declaration** (DGD) for the manifest
- **IMDG segregation compliance** — batteries must be stowed away from foodstuffs and certain other classes

### 4. Commercial / customs paperwork

- Commercial invoice with HS code (typically **8507.10** for SLI flooded, **8507.20** for VRLA / industrial)
- Packing list with battery weight per box
- Certificate of Origin (Form A for GSP-eligible markets)
- Bill of Lading with hazmat class noted

## Common compliance failures

Imports get held at customs or rejected for these issues:

1. **Missing or expired MSDS** — must be within 3 years of issue date
2. **Wrong UN number** — using UN2794 for sealed AGM should be UN2800
3. **Inadequate terminal protection** — exposed terminals can short during transit
4. **Pallet stretchwrap missing electrostatic dissipation** — for some destinations
5. **Container floor not protected** against acid spillage

## Special destination requirements

- **EU** — REACH compliance, battery directive labeling
- **California (US)** — Proposition 65 warning labels
- **GCC countries** — SASO/G-Mark for some categories
- **Brazil** — INMETRO certification often required
- **India** — BIS certification for certain capacity ranges
