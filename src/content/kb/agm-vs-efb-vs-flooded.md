## At a glance

| Technology | Cost Index | Cycle Life (50% DoD) | Best Use Case |
|---|---|---|---|
| Conventional Flooded | 1.0× | 200–400 cycles | Low-cycle starting, refillable service |
| EFB (Enhanced Flooded) | 1.3× | 600–800 cycles | Entry-level start-stop, cost-sensitive markets |
| AGM (Absorbent Glass Mat) | 1.7× | 1000–1200 cycles | Premium start-stop, regen brake, high electrical loads |

## Conventional Flooded

The original lead-acid design — flat plates immersed in liquid sulfuric acid electrolyte. Service-friendly: vent plugs allow electrolyte top-up and hydrometer testing. Cheap to manufacture but low cycle life under deep discharge.

**Pros:** Lowest cost, refillable, field-serviceable, mature recycling chain.
**Cons:** Spillable, gassing under heavy charge, short cycle life in start-stop, plate sulfation risk.

**Right for:** Heavy trucks, agricultural equipment, marine starting, off-grid applications where service is available.

## EFB

EFB adds carbon and graphite to the negative active material — same flooded electrolyte, modified plate chemistry. Doubles cycle life over conventional. Price premium of ~30%.

**Pros:** 2× cycle life of flooded, accepts partial-state-of-charge operation, lower cost than AGM.
**Cons:** Still flooded (gassing potential), not suitable for regen brake systems, slower charge acceptance than AGM.

**Right for:** Entry-level start-stop vehicles, hot climates where AGM degrades, cost-sensitive aftermarket.

## AGM

Fundamentally different design — electrolyte absorbed into fiberglass mats compressed between plates. Sealed, spill-proof, vibration-resistant, and 3–4× the cycle life of flooded.

**Pros:** Spill-proof, high CCA per kg, accepts regen brake charge bursts, deep cycling capable, vibration-resistant.
**Cons:** Most expensive, heat-sensitive (avoid >45°C ambient), cannot top up electrolyte, requires temperature-compensated charging.

**Right for:** European premium start-stop, micro-hybrids, marine deep-cycle, parking AC dual-purpose, RV house banks.

## Decision tree

1. **Vehicle has regenerative braking?** → AGM
2. **Vehicle has start-stop without regen?** → EFB (or AGM if budget allows)
3. **Heavy truck, marine starting, no start-stop?** → Flooded or MF (calcium-calcium)
4. **Solar / off-grid storage?** → VRLA gel deep-cycle (a separate AGM variant)
5. **Parking AC / sleeper cab dual-purpose?** → Heavy-duty AGM (Obsidian Pro class)

## Common pitfalls

- **Replacing AGM with flooded** — start-stop ECU may not detect the wrong battery, but it'll fail in months
- **Using AGM in tropical climates without ventilation** — heat accelerates AGM grid corrosion
- **Mixing AGM and flooded in series** — different charge profiles, fastest battery dies first
- **Using "deep cycle" SLI batteries for solar** — true deep-cycle plate construction is different from automotive deep-cycle marketing
